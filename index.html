❖ Common Name: Olive Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Olea europaea

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Oleaceae

Order: Lamiales

Soil Type:

  • Thrives in well-drained, alkaline, sandy, or rocky soils. Olive trees prefer slightly alkaline to neutral pH and do not tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Life Span:

  • Olive trees are extremely long-lived, often reaching 500 to 1,000 years or more. Some ancient olive trees are believed to be over 2,000 years old.

Pests and Diseases:

  • Pests: Susceptible to pests like olive fruit fly, scales, and aphids, which damage the fruit and leaves.
  • Diseases: Prone to fungal diseases like olive leaf spot, verticillium wilt, and sooty mold, especially in humid conditions.

Medical Uses:

  • Heart Health: Olive oil is widely used to reduce cholesterol and support cardiovascular health.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Olive leaves contain oleuropein, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Antioxidant: Olive oil and leaves are rich in antioxidants, supporting overall immunity and cell health.
  • Diabetes Management: Olive leaf extracts are used in some traditional practices to help manage blood sugar levels.
  • Skin and Hair Care: Olive oil is used extensively in skin and hair care for its moisturizing and antioxidant properties.
